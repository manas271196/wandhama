A nightmare revisited

Reliving the Wandhama massacre of 1998.

On 25th January 1998, Wandhama, a village in central Kashmirâ€™s Ganderbal district witnessed the killing of 23 Kashmiri Hindus in the dead of the night by unidentified gunmen.

The events of that night are recalled by one of the survivors of the incident, a 14-year-old Hindu boy named Vinod Kuman Dhar.

The gunmen came to their house dressed like Indian Army soldiers, had tea with them, waiting for a radio message indicating that all Hindu families in the village had been covered.

After a brief conversation they rounded up all the members of the Hindu households and then gunned them down with Kalashnikov rifles.

The victims included 10 men, 9 women and 4 children. These are their stories.
